About US

Trinity Verd.

Our Services

Our roles in the castor value chain

Managing castor value chain

We manage the castor value chain in 2 counties; Kitui(Kitui West, Kitui East, Kitui Rural, Mwingi North & Mwingi Central) and Makueni(Mbooni West). We are involved with recruiting farmers interested in planting castor, proper training of our farmers to ensure they plant accordingly and purchase of the produce.

Partnership with Farmers

We partner with farmers from the arid and semi-arid areas of Kenya to promote sustainable agricultural practices. Our partnership with the farmers is sealed by a contract dictating we are obligated to purchase the produce once harvested. We have partnerd with over 13,000 farmers across Kitui and Makueni. We train our farmers on proper castor planting techniques and guide them through to harvest.

Monitoring and Evaluation

We monitor the plant from planting period to harvesting period. We visit the farms and check on the germination rate, progress and general health of the plant and give hindsights to the farmer where neccessary. During this phase we watch more on crop environment and pests & diseases control. In the cases where pest and diseases are noticed, we issue the farmers with the appropriate pesticides.

Our Farming Process

How we do our farming

Farmer Recruitment

During this phase we partner with local administrations and organize Barazas where we educate the locals about castor and recruit interested farmers. We record the details of the farmers and stay in constant communication with them.

Seed Distribution.

During the seed distribution phase, we distribute hybrid planting seeds to farmers as we continue to educate them on proper castor farming techniques. More farmers are also recruited during this phase.

Mapping

During this phase, we map the planted acres as we monitor and evaluate the germination rate and the overall health of the crop. We offer hindsights to farmers where neccesary like when weeding is needed.

Harvest and Purchase

At the end of the planting season is the harvest. We educate our farmers on proper harvest and storage techniques in order to avoid post-harvest losses.
After the farmer harvests the castor, we purchase it at competitive prices in both clean and panicle states. We also have decorticators in circulation which help the farmers thresh the castor more effectively.
